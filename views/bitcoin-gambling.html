Bitcoin Gambling

Bitcoin gambling is a general term for any type of gambling wherein bets are placed in units of bitcoins as opposed to a fiat currency or any other assets. Bitcoin gambling is almost exclusively online gambling in bitcoin gambling establishments that allow the depositing, withdrawing and gambling of bitcoins. Bitcoin itself is a virtual currency, that provides numerous advantages to gaming establishments, who can can pass these on to their clientele.


In much of the world, gambling is arduously regulated, heavily taxed and often banned for ethical and religious reasons. Bitcoin however allows gambling establishments to work around or ignore much of these restrictions, primarily as bitcoins are easily and securely transferred without requiring permission from third parties, they are pseudo-anonymous and don’t require providing sensitive information such as credit card numbers or government ID to process.

Many gamblers now prefer to gamble with bitcoin for privacy, cost and security reasons–and buy and sell bitcoins on exchanges, at ATMs or from local dealers on an as-needed basis.

Regulation

Traditionally gamblers have leaned on government regulation in order to ensure that games are fair, and payouts are made. However, in the Wild West of bitcoin gambling instead of regulations, players place greater trust in the gambling operators–and accordingly operators go to great lengths to win gamblers' trust. One of the most common ways to do so is to offer provably fair games, which allow the casino to give a mathematical assurance to the gambler that the game is not rigged. Few gamblers are in a position to validate such a guarantee, but rely on the opinion and general consensus in internet communities.

Bitcoin is tied neither a country nor other currency and as such bitcoin gambling establishments are almost universally global, and as such compete for a global audience. Because of their dramatically reduced cost structure, they are able to compete by offering razor-thin house odds. It is quite typical to find house odds between 0.5 % and 1 %–while a physical casino will have often have games with house odds beyond 15 %. Game Options

Choice is another major driving factor for bitcoin gamblers, with hundreds of games being available, including traditional casino games such as poker and roulette. However, unique to bitcoin are “dice games”, which advocates regard as a very pure form of gambling, while detractors call boring and spreadsheet-like. Dice games allow the gambler to customize their bet size and how much they want to win–and the game calculates a fair probability for them, so that the more they stand to win, the less chance they have of doing so. Because the traditional barriers to entry are removed, a lot of unique games are only available to play with bitcoin (such as bustabit).
